Forces and Newton's Laws of Motion

Physics Project Website

Causes of Motion

Motion is caused when an object experiences a force that changes its state of rest or uniform motion. Without a net external force, objects continue in their current state of motion (inertia).

Definition of a Force

A force is a push or pull upon an object resulting from the object's interaction with another object. It is a vector quantity, having both magnitude and direction.

What Do Forces Do?

Forces can cause objects to accelerate, decelerate, remain in place, or change shape. They are responsible for changes in motion and for maintaining equilibrium.

Categories of Forces

Forces are generally categorized into contact forces (which require physical contact) and non-contact forces (which act at a distance).

Newton's 3 Laws of Motion

First Law (Law of Inertia)

An object remains at rest, or in uniform motion in a straight line, unless acted upon by a net external force.

Free-Body Diagrams (FBDs)

Free-body diagrams (FBDs) are simplified drawings that show all the forces acting on an object. Rules for construction:

  • Draw the object as a simple box or dot.
  • Represent each force as an arrow pointing in the correct direction.
  • Label each arrow with the type of force.
  • The length of the arrow shows the relative strength of the force.
